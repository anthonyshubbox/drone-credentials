⚖️ Lawful Operating Limits (Advanced Operations – Canada)
  • Maximum altitude: 400 feet (120 meters) AGL — higher altitudes permitted only with an RPAS Flight Authorization or SFOC.
  • Visual line-of-sight (VLOS): Drone must remain within unaided sight at all times (BVLOS requires SFOC).
  • Operations near people: Permitted, subject to the aircraft's RPAS Safety Assurance declaration (TP 15263). The DJI Mini 5 Pro's declared assurance level governs permissible distance.
  • Controlled airspace: Permitted with a NAV CANADA RPAS Flight Authorization (via NAV Drone) prior to flight.
  • Airspace coordination: Required near aerodromes, heliports, and Class C/D/E control zones — confirm via NAV Drone before each flight.
  • Night flying: Permitted with anti-collision lighting visible for 3 km.
  • Radio communication: ROC-A (#202604469) authorizes use of aeronautical frequencies (ATC, MF/ATF, UNICOM) where required.
  • Speed limit: No explicit regulatory cap; maintain VLOS and safe operation.
  • Precipitation: Do not fly in rain, snow, or icing conditions (drone is not waterproof).
